About Brain Power

A free cognitive test suite measuring 4 key brain abilities

What is Brain Power?

Brain Power is a free web-based cognitive assessment tool that measures your mental abilities through four scientifically-inspired mini-games. Each game targets a different cognitive domain, and your results are combined into an overall Brain Score on a 1-25 scale.

Our tests are inspired by established cognitive assessment methodologies used in psychology research, including reaction time measurement, working memory span tasks, and visual discrimination tests.

Based on the classic Simon game paradigm, this test measures your working memory capacity. A sequence of colored lights plays, and you must repeat it in order. The sequence grows longer each round, and playback speed increases with level â€” testing both memory span and processing speed.

Short-term MemoryDigit Span

Inspired by the digit span test used in clinical neuropsychology (part of the Wechsler Adult Intelligence Scale). A number is displayed briefly, then you must recall it. Starting at 3 digits, each successful round adds one more digit. Average adults can remember 7Â±2 digits.

Scoring System

Each test produces an ability score on a 1-25 scale:

ScoreLevelDescription
1-5BeginnerBelow average performance
6-10AverageTypical adult performance
11-15Above AverageStrong cognitive performance

23-25ExtraordinaryTheoretical maximum / anomaly detection

Your overall Brain Score is the average of all completed tests. A Deviation Score (similar to IQ standard scoring) is also calculated, centered at 50 with a standard deviation of 10.

How It Works

All data is processed and stored locally in your browser. No personal information is collected or transmitted to any server. Your scores are saved using localStorage, so they persist between sessions on the same device.

The adaptive difficulty system adjusts each test based on your performance, ensuring you're always challenged at an appropriate level.

Disclaimer

Brain Power is an entertainment and self-improvement tool. While our tests are inspired by established cognitive science methodologies, results should not be interpreted as clinical diagnoses or official IQ scores. For professional cognitive assessment, please consult a licensed psychologist.
